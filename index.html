HAOXIN WANG

Research Scientist
Toyota Motor North America R&D - InfoTech Labs

Haoxin Wang is a research scientist at Toyota Motor North America R&D - InfoTech Labs , Mountain View, CA, and is leading the edge computing research project. He received his Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte (UNCC), supervised by Dr. Jiang (Linda) Xie .

His research interests include Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning, Cloud & Edge Computing, AR/VR, and Cooperative Automated Driving.

He is an author of 15+ refereed papers and 15+ U.S. patent applications.

He is serving various roles in multiple academic organizations, including

  • Guest editor of MDPI Electronics Open Access Journal , Special Issue on Machine Learning for Next-Generation Wireless Networks and Computing Systems
  • Technical program committee member in the Eighth International Conference on Big Data, Small Data, Linked Data and Open Data (ALLDATA 2022) and the Seventeenth International Conference on Digital Telecommunications (ICDT 2022)
  • Session chair in the Sixth ACM/IEEE Symposium on Edge Computing (SEC 2021)

Primary Research Area

Cloud/Edge Computing Systems & Internet of Things (IoT)

Edge/Cloud computing provides a hierarchical solution for resource-constrained IoT, mobile, and embedded devices to offload their computation-intensive tasks, and thus to achieve high performance, real-time, and low-cost services. Applications of cloud/edge computing include (but not limited to): data offloading, video analytics, smart home, smart city, connected health, intelligent transportation systems, etc.

Research Detail

Artificial Intelligence (AI) & Machine Learning

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning are making it easier for people to do things every day, such as Tesla Autopilot, Google Assistant, and Apple Face ID. Besides these everyday applications, AI and machine learning also provide new ways of looking at existing problems, for example, complex and large-scale intelligent systems design, management, and maintenance; cybersecurity; and robotics.

Research Detail

AR/VR & Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

The field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is focused on improving the efficiency and effectiveness of human-computer interfaces through the development of novel software and hardware designs to recognize and interpret human characteristics and behavior. Improvements in HCI technology can lead to enhanced AR/VR experiences by providing more natural and efficient ways for a user to interact with a real or virtual environment.

Research Detail

Next-Gen Heterogeneous Wireless Networks

Heterogeneous Networks refer to a complex eco system of radio cells with varying coverage ranges and technologies that interoperate to deliver the required capacity and coverage. In the future, multiple wireless communication technologies will co-exist, such as 5G, DSRC, Wi-Fi, V2X, to support a variety of emerging applications including AR/VR, connected and autonomous vehicles, IoT, edge AI, etc.
